History of Lake Brushes

Lake Brush, also known as "Lake Ying", is produced in Huzhou City, Zhejiang Province, and has the reputation of "the crown of brushes". The history of Lake Brush production can be traced back to the Spring and Autumn and Warring States periods more than 2,000 years ago. It was already famous during the Tang Dynasty and reached its peak during the Ming and Qing dynasties.

Shanlian Town, as the birthplace of Lake Brushes, has been an important production base for brushes among the Four Treasures of the Study since ancient times. The local brush-making craftsmanship has been passed down through generations, forming the unique Lake Brush production techniques, which have been listed as National Intangible Cultural Heritage.

"The finest brush craftsmanship under heaven" - this is the highest praise from ancient people for the quality of Lake Brushes.

Traditional Brush-Making Process

The production of Lake Brushes is extremely complex, requiring more than 120 processes including material selection, water basin work, dry work, and brush assembly. Each process requires exquisite skills and rich experience.

1

Material Selection

Carefully select premium animal hair materials such as goat hair, weasel hair, rabbit hair, etc.

2

Water Basin Work

Clean, classify, and organize the hair materials, removing impurities

3

Dry Work

Transform processed hair materials into brush heads, including hair blending and shaping

4

Brush Assembly

Install the brush head into the handle, completing the entire brush construction

Brush Usage & Maintenance

Proper usage and maintenance methods can greatly extend the lifespan of brushes and maintain good writing effects.

First-time Use

  • New brushes need opening before use, soak the brush head slowly in warm water
  • Don't pull the brush hair forcefully, let it spread naturally
  • Opening time usually takes 10-30 minutes

Daily Usage

  • Keep the brush handle vertical during use, control the brush tip with wrist strength
  • Dip appropriate amount of ink, avoid excessive ink affecting writing
  • Write with proper pressure to protect the brush tip

Cleaning & Care

  • Clean with clear water immediately after use
  • Gently press the brush head to remove excess water and reshape
  • Hang or lay flat to air dry, avoid direct sunlight
